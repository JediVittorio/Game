# Era 3: Last Fantasy Strategies
**Baseline Title:** FFT (1997)
**STATUS:** EXPANDED (Stage 2 Initialized)

## Phase 1 (Satire): Zoning Laws
Arguing over property rights and filing Form 8B for a Z-Axis.

## Stage 2 (Serious): The Geometric Prisons
The "Grids" are literal data-containers where the user's personality traits are being sorted and archived. The "Zoning Disputes" are the UDE's way of preventing different parts of the user's mind from communicating. 

**Romza** isn't archaic because of bad localization; he is the user's "Logical Core" trying to use complex language to bypass the simulation's censorship. The "Zodiac Stones" are actually physical memory modules being scavenged by the UDE.

# Era 4: Ultimate Illusion X
**Baseline Title:** FFX (2001)
**STATUS:** EXPANDED (Stage 2 Initialized)

## Phase 1 (Satire): Forced Laughter & Unions
Apocalyptic laughter and spreadsheet-based summoner strikes.

## Stage 2 (Serious): The Emotional Exhaustion
The "Sonic Laugh" is a defense mechanism against the "Quiet," the real-world silence of the hospital room where the user is dying. The "Summon Union" is a manifestation of the user's failing neural pathways—the brain's refusal to keep processing the simulation's visual load. 

**Tadpole** is a reflection of the user's own father, a man whose "forced optimism" during a family tragedy caused a permanent rift. **Luna** is the user's subconscious awareness of their own terminal status.

# Era 5: Origami Maurice
**Baseline Title:** Paper Mario (2004)
**STATUS:** EXPANDED (Stage 2 Initialized)

## Phase 1 (Satire): Cardboard Awareness
The boss realizes he is made of paper and refuses to leave his box.

## Stage 2 (Serious): The Fragility of Identity
The "Paper" aesthetic is the simulation's way of dealing with extremely low data bandwidth. In Stage 2, the "Riot" is the sound of the user's real-world heart monitor failing. 

The **Demon** in the box is the user's core childhood trauma, something so "fragile" that the mind has flattened it into a two-dimensional joke to avoid the pain of 3D reality. The "Millennial Portal" is the exit to the simulation, which the UDE has "blocked" with the user's own fears.
