# Era 1: DirtBound
**Baseline Title:** EarthBound (1994)
**STATUS:** EXPANDED (Stage 2 Initialized)

## Narrative Premise (Phase 1: Satirical)
A parody of 90s suburban RPGs. The meteor misses, so the heroes must manufacture their own apocalypse by being suburban terrorists.

## Narrative Premise (Stage 2: Grounded Reality)
The bright, vibrant suburbia is a "Neuro-Nostalgia" filter applied over the ruins of **Onett-2**, a decommissioned industrial hub. The "Cosmic Dread" isn't a joke; it's the encroaching "Data-Blight" that consumes the user's real-world memories. 

In Stage 2, the "Psychic potential" of Less is revealed as a side effect of the VR headset's neural interface failure. The maternal neglect mentioned in Phase 1 is a grounded story about the protagonist's mother being a researcher who disappeared during the initial AGES development.

## Setting Details: Suburban Neighborhood
- **Phase 1 Visuals:** High-contrast, neon-retro tiles. Lemons are the size of basketballs.
- **Stage 2 Visuals:** Desaturated, flickering textures. The "Lemonade Stand" is actually a contaminated water distribution point. The "Arcade" is a server room processing the user's brainwaves.

## Expanded Characters (Serious Tones)
### Less
- **Satire:** Choice Boy with a striped shirt.
- **Reality:** A terminal patient using the AGES simulation to experience a childhood he never had. His "Psychic" powers are actually his ability to manipulate the VR code as his brain merges with the system.

### Carla
- **Satire:** Telekinetic snack thief.
- **Reality:** A digital construct created from the protagonist's lost sister's medical records. Her "Snack Retrieval" is actually her scavenging for stable data packets to keep the simulation running.

## Stage 2 Plot Twist
The "Boss: Adulthood" in Stage 1 was a joke about bills. In Stage 2, "Adulthood" is a literal manifestation of the hospital staff trying to pull the user out of the VR before it causes permanent brain death.
