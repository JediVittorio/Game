# Era 5: Origami Maurice — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The 2.5D Duo)

### 1. Maurice (The Mute Plumber)
*   **Visuals:** A stout man in red overalls and a flat cap. He is literally two-dimensional, appearing as a thin line when he turns around. His mustache is made of black felt.
*   **Personality:** Maurice is entirely mute. He communicates through a series of expressive gestures and by folding himself into various origami shapes (a swan for "Yes," a crumpled ball for "No"). He possesses a deep, existential dread of rain.
*   **Narrative Role:** The "hero" who possesses an array of hammers and boots but is primarily valued for his ability to slip through the gaps in the game's logic.
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Special Move" involves him realizing he can just step *between* the 3D planes of the game world, making him briefly invincible but very confused.

### 2. Shell-don (The Anxious Researcher)
*   **Visuals:** A turtle wearing a blue helmet and carrying a massive, encyclopedic tome that is larger than his own shell. He wears thick, square-rimmed glasses.
*   **Personality:** Shell-don is a professional pessimist. He spends every combat encounter reading his book to find enemy weaknesses, usually concluding that the situation is "statistically hopeless."
*   **Narrative Role:** The source of all lore and technical data. He breaks the fourth wall to complain about "Glitches" and "Frame Rate Drops."
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Tattle" ability not only reveals HP but also reveals the enemy's tragic childhood trauma, making the player feel guilty for attacking them.

## Major NPCs

### 3. The Shadow Cardboard (The Boss)
*   **Description:** An ancient demon made of high-quality, reinforced corrugated cardboard. It has glowing purple eyes and a cape made of bubble wrap.
*   **Role:** The "Final Boss" who is paralyzed by a fear of humidity and the recycling bin.
*   **Dialogue:** "You think you can defeat me? I am double-walled! I am acid-free! Stay away with that fire! Do you know how much a papercut hurts on a metaphysical level?"

### 4. The Grumpy Critic
*   **Description:** A silhouetted figure in the front row of the audience, holding a "0/10" sign.
*   **Role:** An interactive audience member who triggers the "Riot" mechanic if the player uses the same move twice.
*   **Dialogue:** "Derivative! I've seen this combo in Era 1! Bring out the flaming anvils! This production is a waste of my tax-sheltered income!"

### 5. Admiral Bob-ery (The Explosive Retiree)
*   **Description:** An elderly, round bomb with a massive white mustache and a sailor's hat.
*   **Role:** The "Slightly Unstable" companion. He represents the era's obsession with tragic backstories.
*   **Dialogue:** "I lost my fuse in the Great Origami War of '94... now I just explode whenever someone mentions my late wife. BOOM! (Sobs in paper)."

## Common Enemies (The Papercut Posse)

*   **Art Critic:** A tall, thin man with a monocle and a scathing review. It attacks by "Diminishing" the player's stats with insults.
*   **Flaming Anvil:** A literal stage prop that falls from the sky. It deals massive damage and inflicts "Flattened."
*   **Faded Goomba:** A brown mushroom-thing that is so sun-bleached it's almost white. It attacks out of a desire for relevance.
*   **Cardstock Koopa:** A turtle whose shell is made of heavy-duty paper. It deals bonus damage if it slides on a "Polished Floor" tile.
*   **Existential Cardboard:** A sentient box that traps the player inside and forces them to contemplate their own fragility.
