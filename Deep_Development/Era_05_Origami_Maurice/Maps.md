# Era 5: Origami Maurice — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Kingdom of Cardboard & Creases
Era 5 is a world that is literally 2.5D. Characters and environmental objects are paper-thin, casting long, flat shadows on a world that looks like a high-budget school play. The air smells like library paste and fresh cardstock. The sound of scissors cutting through air occasionally echoes in the distance.

---

## 1. Rogue-ish Port: The Hub of Humidity
A gritty, seedy town made of damp, recycled cardboard. The buildings are held together by giant pieces of masking tape that have started to peel.

### Map 5.1: The Theater Square
*   **Atmosphere:** Sounds of a restless audience and the constant rustle of paper.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Millennial Portal:** A massive, glowing door drawn in marker on a particularly thick piece of cardboard. It leads to the "Under-Folder."
    *   **The Hanging Noose:** A comedic, flat prop in the center of town that reminds everyone of the era's "edge."
*   **NPC Placement:** **Maurice** is standing near a "Help Wanted" sign, folding himself into different shapes to amuse the local paper-crowd.

---

## 2. The Great Tree of Cellulose: The Static Forest
A forest made of green tissue paper and brown construction paper. The leaves are attached with visible staples.

### Map 5.2: The Puni-ish Village
*   **Atmosphere:** High-pitched, synthesized squeaking. The lighting is artificial, suggesting a stage spotlight.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Static Field:** An area where walking generates "Static Electricity" that causes the player's hair to stand up (a mechanical debuff to Evasion).
    *   **The Secret Pipe:** A green plastic tube that looks completely out of place in a paper world.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles with "Faded Goombas" and "Cardstock Koopas."

---

## 3. The Glitz-ish Pit: The Arena of Critique
A massive theater where fighters compete for "Audience Approval" and "Star Points."

### Map 5.3: The Backstage Labyrinth
*   **Atmosphere:** Claustrophobic. Smells of greasepaint and nervous sweat.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Prop Storage:** Contains giant hammers, oversized boots, and "Emotional Backstory" scripts.
    *   **The Critic's Box:** A high balcony where the audience throws flaming anvils at the "bad" performers.
*   **Gameplay:** A series of puzzle rooms where Maurice must "Fold" himself to slip through cracks in the stage scenery.

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Millennial Fortress
A sprawling castle made of reinforced, double-walled corrugated cardboard. It is surprisingly sturdy but weak to fire.

### Map 5.4: Floor 1 — The Hall of Papercuts
*   **Atmosphere:** Dangerous. The walls are sharpened to a razor edge.
*   **Gimmick:** "The Humidity Trap." Steam pipes (from the real world?) threaten to make the party "Soggy," reducing their physical attack to zero.

### Map 5.5: The Shadow Queen’s Box (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A darkened stage with a single, massive cardboard box in the center.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Cardboard Throne:** Where the boss hides, terrified of the recycling bin.
    *   **The Audience Rows:** Visible in the foreground. If they aren't entertained, they throw "Maximum Damage" debris.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The player must perform QTEs to "Entertain" the audience while fighting the boss. If the audience is happy, they heal the player. If they are bored, they join the boss.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Theater Stage
*   **Aesthetic:** A wooden stage with painted backdrops that fall down when a character dies.
*   **Background:** Rows of silhouetted audience members whose eyes glow in the dark.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Art Critics," "Flaming Anvils," and "Existential Cardboard."
*   **Gimmick:** "The Stage Hazard." Occasionally, a piece of the set falls on a random combatant, dealing heavy damage.
