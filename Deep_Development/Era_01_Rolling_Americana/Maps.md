# Era 1: Rolling Americana — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: Suburbia Prime
Suburbia Prime is a town that feels like a pastel-colored dream of the 1990s, where the sun is always at a 45-degree angle and the clouds look suspiciously like cotton candy. Everything is "pleasant" to a degree that induces a low-level, persistent headache. The streets are paved with a pristine gray that has never seen a pothole, and the white picket fences are sharpened to a point that suggests more than just decorative intent.

---

## 1. Suburbia Prime: Residential District
The heart of the "boring Tuesday." This area is a sprawl of identical two-story houses with manicured lawns that look like they were trimmed with nail scissors.

### Map 1.1: Less’s House (The Anchor of Neglect)
*   **Atmosphere:** Smells of lemon furniture polish and unresolved emotional debt. The lighting is warm but feels hollow.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Living Room:** Features a television that only plays white noise and a telephone that rings exactly once every six hours.
    *   **The Kitchen:** A pristine space where a single plate of "Generic Nutritional Round-Things" sits on the table.
    *   **Less’s Bedroom:** A shrine to late-20th-century consumerism. A PC that takes ten minutes to boot up, a collection of comic books featuring heroes who never actually win, and a window that looks out onto a street where nothing ever happens.
*   **NPC Placement:** **Mom** is perpetually standing by the stove, stirring a pot that contains only water and optimism.

### Map 1.2: The Spiteful Neighbor’s Yard
*   **Atmosphere:** Tense. The grass is slightly greener here, which is an insult to the entire neighborhood.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Prize Rose Bushes:** These are the primary targets for Less’s "escalation." They are unnaturally red and seem to pulse with a faint, anxious rhythm.
    *   **The Dog House:** Home to a dog named "Litigation" who barks in Morse code.

## 2. Suburbia Prime: Commercial District
Where the "pleasantness" becomes a commercial imperative. The music here is a slightly-too-fast MIDI version of a elevator jazz track.

### Map 1.3: The Library of Chronic Anxiety
*   **Atmosphere:** Heavy, silent, and smelling of old glue. The shelves are packed with books, but none of them have titles on the spine.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The "Silent" Zone:** An area where the player’s movement speed is halved, and any sound triggers a "Shushing" animation from invisible librarians.
    *   **The Restricted Section:** Contains books like *1001 Ways to Disappoint Your Parents* and *The Geometry of Tax Evasion*.
*   **Gameplay:** This serves as a "stealth" map where the player must retrieve the "Tax Fraud Documentation" without being shushed into oblivion.

### Map 1.4: The Arcade of Broken Dreams (The Lemonade Front)
*   **Atmosphere:** Flashing neon lights that flicker in patterns that cause mild nausea. The sound of synthesized bleeps and bloops competes with the aggressive pouring of lemonade.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Lemonade Stand:** A massive, mahogany structure in the center of the arcade, manned by children in business suits. It is the most profitable enterprise in town.
    *   **The "Unbeatable" Cabinet:** A game called *Life Simulator* where the player character just waits for a bus that never arrives.
*   **NPC Placement:** **The Lemonade Leader** stands atop a pile of lemon crates, surveying his empire.

---

## 3. The Dungeon: The Office of Mundanity
The ultimate destination for those who have generated enough cosmic dread. It is a vertical tower of beige cubicles.

### Map 1.5: Floor 1 — The Lobby of Paperwork
*   **Atmosphere:** Harsh fluorescent lighting and the sound of a thousand mechanical keyboards clicking out of sync.
*   **Gimmick:** The "Permit Gate." The player must interact with five different desks to get five different stamps to open the elevator. Each desk NPC asks a nonsensical question about their childhood.

### Map 1.6: The Executive Suite (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A vast, empty room with a floor made of polished obsidian (or just really dark linoleum). A single, massive desk sits at the far end.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Windows:** Look out into a starless, beige void.
    *   **The Desk:** Features a nameplate that says "MR. ADULTHOOD."
*   **Boss Map Logic:** During the fight, the cubicles in the background slowly close in, shrinking the battle area and increasing the "Claustrophobia" meter.

## 4. Battle Maps: The Grassy Void
*   **Aesthetic:** A rolling field of hyper-green grass under a sky that is a permanent, unnerving shade of cyan.
*   **Encounters:** This is where random battles with "Aged Children," "Stray Thoughts," and "Manic Mailmen" occur. The background of the battle features shifting, psychedelic patterns that resemble a kaleidoscope made of suburban nightmares.
