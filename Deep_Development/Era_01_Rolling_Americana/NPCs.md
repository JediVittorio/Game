# Era 1: Rolling Americana — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Trio of Suburban Terror)

### 1. Less (The Chosen Boy)
*   **Visuals:** A young boy wearing a striped yellow-and-blue shirt that is exactly one size too small. His eyes are large, vacant, and reflect a terrifying lack of internal monologue. He carries a cracked plastic bat that he refers to as "Justice."
*   **Personality:** Less communicates primarily through silent, judgmental stares and occasional, monosyllabic affirmations. He is the personification of "going through the motions." He isn't a hero; he's just a child whose mother forgot to tell him that he isn't special.
*   **Narrative Role:** The "corporate strikebreaker" in training. He is the one who initiates the suburban terrorism required to advance the plot.
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Homecoming" mechanic is triggered not by homesickness, but by a sudden realization of how much his mortgage will eventually be.

### 2. Carla (The Telekinetic Glutton)
*   **Visuals:** A girl in a pink dress with a red bow that looks like it’s strangling her hair. She floats two inches off the ground, not because of her powers, but because she refuses to touch the "common dirt."
*   **Personality:** Carla possesses the power to reshape the universe, which she uses exclusively to bring snacks from the kitchen to the couch without standing up. She speaks in high-pitched, breathy demands and views everyone else as a delivery service.
*   **Narrative Role:** The magical "heavy hitter" who refuses to use her powers unless a snack is involved. Her "PSI Fire" is actually just her heating up a frozen burrito to a temperature that can melt steel.

### 3. Geoff (The Violator of Treaties)
*   **Visuals:** A boy with glasses so thick they could act as magnifying glasses for a death ray. He wears a lab coat over his pajamas and carries a backpack that hums with the sound of a miniature fission reactor.
*   **Personality:** Geoff is a "genius" whose intellect is focused entirely on the most efficient ways to violate international laws regarding toy manufacturing. He speaks in rapid-fire technical jargon that he clearly doesn't understand.
*   **Narrative Role:** The gadgeteer whose inventions are 90% likely to explode and 100% likely to result in a lawsuit. He considers the Geneva Convention to be a "suggestion list."

## Major NPCs

### 4. Mom (The Financial Ghost)
*   **Description:** A tall, slender woman who is perpetually turned away from the camera, stirring a pot. She never moves. She never blinks.
*   **Role:** She serves as the player’s "Bank." She does not offer love, comfort, or advice. She only acknowledges Less's existence when he deposits currency.
*   **Satire:** Her dialogue is limited to financial summaries. "Hello, Less. You have deposited 500 dollars. I have put 10% into a high-yield savings account for your eventual therapy. Goodbye."

### 5. The Lemonade Leader (Frank Fly Satire)
*   **Description:** A twelve-year-old boy in a three-piece suit, standing atop a mountain of lemon crates in the arcade. He holds a gold-plated juicer like a scepter.
*   **Role:** The first "Boss" of the era. He represents the terrifying efficiency of youth-led capitalism.
*   **Dialogue:** "You think you can just kick over my stand? My margins are protected by three layers of shell companies and a very aggressive PAC. You’re not a hero, kid. You’re a market fluctuation."

### 6. The Panic-Stricken Neighbor
*   **Description:** A middle-aged man in a bathrobe, perpetually watering his lawn with a hose that has no water coming out.
*   **Role:** The primary victim of the player's "escalation." He exists solely to be terrorized.
*   **Satire:** His dialogue reflects the absolute peak of suburban anxiety. "The HOA is going to see those roses, Less! They have satellites! They have drones! Why are you looking at me like that? WHY IS YOUR BAT HUMMING?"

## Common Enemies (The Suburbia Prime Denizens)

*   **Aged Child:** A small boy with a beard and a briefcase. He attacks by complaining about his 401k.
*   **Stray Thought:** A floating, translucent cloud that whispers things like "Did you leave the stove on?" or "Everyone is laughing at your hair."
*   **Manic Mailman:** A man in a blue uniform who tries to force-feed you junk mail and "urgent" bills.
*   **The HOA Inspector:** A woman with a clipboard who deals damage by measuring the height of your grass and finding it "insufficient."
