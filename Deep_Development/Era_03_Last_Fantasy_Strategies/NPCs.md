# Era 3: Last Fantasy Strategies — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Tacticians of Red Tape)

### 1. Romza (The Verbose Heretic)
*   **Visuals:** A young noble with blonde hair tied in a ponytail and a tabard featuring a shield split by a quill. He carries a rapier but prefers to fight with paragraphs.
*   **Personality:** Romza is the personification of a 1990s localization effort. He speaks exclusively in archaic, overly complex English. He cannot say "Hello" when he can say "I beseech thee, traveler of the dusty road, to lend thy weary ears to my humble greeting."
*   **Narrative Role:** The "hero" who is constantly being accused of heresy, which in this era means "failing to file a change of address form."
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Ultimate Skill" is a speech that is so long it physically pushes enemies off the isometric grid.

### 2. The Bureaucrat (The Administrative Support)
*   **Visuals:** A man in a high-collared robe with ink stains on his fingers and a massive scroll-case strapped to his back. He carries a sharpened quill and a bottle of "Permanent Ink."
*   **Personality:** He believes that the universe is governed by laws, and if a person is not properly registered, they do not technically exist. He is the only person who can fast-track Romza's magic permits.
*   **Narrative Role:** The healer/support unit. He doesn't cast "Cure"; he files a "Health Requisition Form."

### 3. Agrias (The Overqualified Guard)
*   **Visuals:** A female knight in heavy, ornate plate armor. She has a stern expression and carries a massive holy sword that hums with the sound of a choir of accountants.
*   **Personality:** She is deeply loyal to the crown, but primarily because she has a very competitive retirement plan. She is frustrated by the fact that she has to wait her turn (CT) to execute "Holy Explosion."
*   **Narrative Role:** The tank. She protects Romza by standing on the correct tile and looking dignified.

## Major NPCs

### 4. Marquis de Merce (The Final Auditor)
*   **Description:** A tall, slender man with a silver goatee and a robe made of recycled tax documents. He carries a scepter shaped like a giant fountain pen.
*   **Role:** The "Villain" of the era. He represents the absolute power of institutional bureaucracy.
*   **Dialogue:** "Thou hast entered my domain without a signed waiver? Thine existence is an audit-risk! I shall strike thy name from the ledger of the living and reclassify thy soul as 'Miscellaneous Business Expense'!"

### 5. Gafgarion (The Mercenary of Convenience)
*   **Description:** A dark knight with a sinister helmet and a penchant for stealing the player’s lunch money.
*   **Role:** A recurring antagonist who switches sides based on whoever offers a better dental plan.
*   **Dialogue:** "Blood? Honor? Hah! I fight for the highest bidder, and the Marquis offers a 401k match that would make a saint weep. Prepare to be downsized, Romza!"

### 6. The High Priest of Zoning
*   **Description:** An elderly man in golden robes who sits atop a throne made of law books.
*   **Role:** The moral authority who determines what is "Heretical" (anything that decreases property value).
*   **Dialogue:** "The Zodiac Audits speak of a time when man built houses without permits! Such chaos must never return! To the Deep Dungeon of Debt with thee!"

## Common Enemies (The Civil Servants)

*   **Heretical Knight:** A soldier who forgot to polish their boots according to the royal decree.
*   **Tax Collector:** A small, fast enemy that drains the player’s gold instead of HP.
*   **Audit Demon:** A creature made of ink and shadows that attacks by revealing the player's past mistakes.
*   **Zoned Chocobo:** A large, flightless bird wearing a high-visibility vest. It only moves in straight lines.
