# Era 3: Last Fantasy Strategies — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Grid-Locked Kingdom of Ivalies
Era 3 is a world of rigid lines, high-elevation drama, and crushing bureaucracy. The landscape is viewed exclusively from an isometric perspective, where even the grass grows in perfect 1x1 squares. The color palette is muted—sepia, deep browns, and faded stone—reflecting the "seriousness" of the municipal disputes.

---

## 1. The City of Lesalia: Municipal Center
The capital city, where every cobblestone has been audited for structural integrity and political alignment.

### Map 3.1: The Town Square of Trivial Disputes
*   **Atmosphere:** Smells of damp parchment and ink. The background music is a somber, orchestral track dominated by low-register cellos.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Proclamation Board:** Filled with notices about zoning changes, parking fines, and heretical tax brackets.
    *   **The Fountain of Red Tape:** A stone fountain where the water flows in a very specific, regulated pattern.
*   **NPC Placement:** **Romza** is standing in front of a group of soldiers, delivering a 15-minute monologue about the sanctity of property lines.

---

## 2. The Plains of Zeklaus: The Disputed Plot
A vast, open field that looks like a giant chessboard. This is the primary battlefield for the "Zoning Wars."

### Map 3.2: The Agricultural Grid
*   **Atmosphere:** Windy and austere. The grid lines are physically visible on the ground.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Forbidden Tile:** A single tile in the center of the field that is zoned for "Sacred Meditation." Stepping on it results in an immediate 1,000 Gold fine.
    *   **The Ruined Barn:** A strategic high-point that provides a +10% Accuracy bonus but a -15% Charisma penalty for trespassing.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles here are slow. Characters must wait for their "Permits" (CT) to execute any action.

---

## 3. The Monastery of Orbonne: The Archives of Audits
A massive stone structure built on a cliffside. It houses the records of every financial transaction in the history of the kingdom.

### Map 3.3: The Great Library of Ledgers
*   **Atmosphere:** Claustrophobic. The shelves reach so high they disappear into the sepia-toned mist.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Zodiac Stone Vault:** A high-security room containing glowing stones that look like paperweights. They are actually the "Zodiac Audits."
    *   **The Floating Platforms:** Isometric steps that require the "Jump" stat to navigate.
*   **NPC Placement:** **The Bureaucrat** is frantically filing away the player's tax returns.

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Deep Dungeon of Debt
A subterranean labyrinth where those who cannot pay their municipal fines are sent.

### Map 3.4: Floor 1 — The Hall of Interest Rates
*   **Atmosphere:** Dark, damp, and echoes with the sound of a thousand quills scratching on parchment.
*   **Gimmick:** "The Compounding Debt." Every turn the player takes in this dungeon increases the "Debt" meter. If the meter reaches 100%, the party is forcibly converted into "Unpaid Intern" enemy types.

### Map 3.5: The Final Audit Chamber (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A circular room with a massive, golden scale in the center.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Ink Well of Eternity:** A pool of black ink that the boss uses to rewrite the player's stats.
    *   **The Floating Scrolls:** These orbit the boss, providing shielding based on how many items the player has in their inventory.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The boss, **Marquis de Merce**, changes the height of the grid tiles every three turns, forcing the player to constantly adjust their "Jump" strategy.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Isometric Void
*   **Aesthetic:** A floating platform made of mismatched stone tiles suspended in a sepia-colored abyss.
*   **Background:** Faintly visible diagrams of city planning and tax codes.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Heretical Knights," "Tax Collectors," and "Wild Chocobos" (who are actually just stray chickens that were improperly zoned).
