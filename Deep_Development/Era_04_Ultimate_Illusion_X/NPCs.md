# Era 4: Ultimate Illusion X — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Underwater Debtors)

### 1. Tadpole (The Sonic Athlete)
*   **Visuals:** A young man wearing an aggressively asymmetrical outfit. One pant leg is long and baggy; the other is a short-short. He has more belts than actual clothing. His hair is a shade of blonde that only exists in hair salons and nightmares.
*   **Personality:** Tadpole is a professional athlete whose primary skill is "Loud Optimism." He suffers from severe paternal trauma, which he compensates for by laughing so loudly it breaks windows.
*   **Narrative Role:** The "hero" who is technically a ghost/memory/sportsperson. He provides the physical damage and the environmental destruction via his "Sonic Laugh."
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Overdrive" is called "Blitzball Spreadsheet," where he attacks the enemy with 10% accuracy but 100% tax liability.

### 2. Luna (The Union Summoner)
*   **Visuals:** A young woman in a multi-layered dress that looks like it would be a death trap in any body of water. She carries a staff with a seashell on the end.
*   **Personality:** Luna speaks in soft, breathy whispers about "The Spirits of the Water" and "The Spiral of Sunk Costs." She is surprisingly adept at labor law and spends most of her time negotiating with the entities she summons.
*   **Narrative Role:** The magical support. She doesn't just "summon" Aeons; she calls them in from their union-mandated break.
*   **Satirical Twist:** Her "Grand Summon" requires the player to first pay a "Summoner’s License Fee" using the party’s gold.

## Major NPCs

### 3. The Maritime CEO (Sin Satire)
*   **Description:** A gargantuan whale-like entity with a single, massive eye and a body made of sunken ships and luxury real estate.
*   **Role:** The "Final Boss" (temporarily). It represents the crushing weight of systemic maritime debt.
*   **Dialogue:** "I am the spiral! I am the debt! I am the inevitable result of interest rates compounding over a thousand years! Have you brought the hazard pay?"

### 4. Auron-ish (The Cynical Guardian)
*   **Description:** A middle-aged man with a permanent scowl, one arm tucked into his coat, and a massive jug of "Liquid Cynicism" strapped to his waist.
*   **Role:** The mentor who has seen the ending of the game three times and is deeply bored by the dialogue.
*   **Dialogue:** "The pilgrimage is just a subscription service for the afterlife, Tadpole. Pay your dues or die. I’m only here for the retirement benefits."

### 5. The Union Rep (The Fayth Satire)
*   **Description:** A small child sitting in a stone throne, holding a clipboard and looking extremely tired.
*   **Role:** The gatekeeper to the summons. He represents the "Voice of the Workers."
*   **Dialogue:** "Bahamut is on his fifteen-minute lunch break. If you want him to attack the whale, you'll need to sign these three forms and agree to a 5% increase in his pension fund."

## Common Enemies (The Aquatic Audits)

*   **Maritime Insurance Agent:** A humanoid fish in a business suit. It attacks by "Underwriting" the player, preventing them from healing for 3 turns.
*   **Underwater Grunt:** A standard armored soldier who is visibly struggling to breathe. It deals "Desperation Damage."
*   **Jellyfish Litigator:** A translucent, stinging entity that applies "Stun" and "Lawsuit" status effects.
*   **Whale-Sized Variance:** A massive, blubbery creature that deals damage based on how much the player’s gold count differs from their last turn.
