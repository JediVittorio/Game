# Era 4: Ultimate Illusion X — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Archipelago of Sunk Costs
Era 4 is a world of blindingly bright sunlight, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and the constant, crushing sound of forced laughter. The aesthetic is tropical "post-apocalyptic"—vibrant reefs grow over the ruins of old spreadsheets, and the air smells like coconut oil and maritime insurance.

---

## 1. Besaid-ish Village: The Departure Point
A series of huts built on stilts over a shallow lagoon. The water is so clear it’s visually distracting.

### Map 4.1: The Beach of Resignation
*   **Atmosphere:** Sounds of crashing waves and the distant chanting of a labor union.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Sending Circle:** A platform where **Luna** performs her "Sending," which in this era looks like she’s aggressively filing a series of exit interviews for the deceased.
    *   **The Training Ground:** Where **Tadpole** practices his Blitzball kicks, occasionally hitting a passing seagull.
*   **NPC Placement:** **Tadpole** is standing near the water, practicing his "Sonic Laugh" while the local villagers wear noise-canceling earmuffs.

---

## 2. The City of Luca-ish: The Spreadsheet Dome
A massive, high-tech stadium built entirely around the mechanics of a sport that no one understands.

### Map 4.2: The Blitzball Finance Office
*   **Atmosphere:** Fluorescent lighting and the frantic clicking of abacuses.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Water Sphere:** A giant floating ball of water where athletes fight over salaries.
    *   **The Merchandise Stall:** Sells "Tadpole Laughter" in a bottle (it’s just a recording of a sonic boom).
*   **Gameplay:** This is a non-combat map where the player must navigate a series of dialogue trees to secure sponsorship for their unionized summons.

---

## 3. The Pilgrimage Path: The Spiral of Fees
A long, winding mountain path that leads to the final temple. Every step feels like it costs money.

### Map 4.3: The Highroad of Tolls
*   **Atmosphere:** Dramatic cliffs and sweeping vistas, ruined by the presence of a toll booth every 50 meters.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Al Bhed Picket Line:** A group of tech-savvy individuals holding signs that say "GIVE US BACK OUR SPRITES."
    *   **The Chocobo Rental Station:** Where the birds charge a premium rate for "nostalgic transportation."
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles with "Maritime Insurance Agents" and "Underwater Grunts."

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Union Hall of Summons
A grand, underwater temple where the ultimate entities reside. It is currently under a "Do Not Enter" strike order.

### Map 4.4: Floor 1 — The Chamber of Benefits
*   **Atmosphere:** Opulent, dripping with water and luxury.
*   **Gimmick:** "The Union Negotiation." The player must find three "Hazard Pay Vouchers" hidden in various trial rooms to convince the summons to stop picketing the boss's arena.

### Map 4.5: The Core of the Whale (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** Inside the stomach of a gargantuan maritime entity. It looks like a high-end corporate boardroom.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The CEO’s Chair:** Where the boss sits, surrounded by stacks of gold.
    *   **The Digestive Fluids:** Occasionally rise, dealing "Corrosive Tax" damage to the party.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The player must use **Luna’s** "Sending" to banish the boss's liquid assets to the afterlife, weakening its defenses.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Tropical Reef
*   **Aesthetic:** Glowing coral, schools of neon fish, and floating debris from sunken sports equipment.
*   **Background:** A shimmering underwater horizon with rays of sun-god light piercing the surface.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Deep Sea Debtors," "Jellyfish Litigators," and "Whale-Sized Variances."
