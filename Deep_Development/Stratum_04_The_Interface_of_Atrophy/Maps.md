# Stratum 4: The Interface of Atrophy — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Rotting Code
Stratum 4 is a descent into the literal architecture of the game. The aesthetic shifts from physical stone and flesh to a digital void of floating hexadecimal strings, broken UI elements, and "Scanline" clouds. The world is breaking down. Walls are made of scrolling "ERROR" messages, and the ground is a flickering grid of 1s and 0s.

---

## Floor 16: Meta-Weight
*   **Atmosphere:** Glitchy and unstable. The background music is a distorted version of the Title Screen theme.
*   **Layout:** A large room with a single massive pressure plate (Weight 50) and a heavy door.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Meta-Menu Pedestal:** An interactable object that opens a "Physical Menu."
*   **Gimmick:** The room is empty of pushable blocks. To solve the puzzle, the player must open their "Character Menu," select their **Max HP Bar** (a visual UI element), and use the **Push** verb to drag it onto the map as a physical, 2x2 object. Doing so permanently reduces Max HP by 20%.

---

## Floor 17: The Hostile Pause
*   **Atmosphere:** High-pressure and claustrophobic. The air hums with the sound of an approaching server crash.
*   **Layout:** A simple corridor filled with "Hanging Assets"—monsters that drop from the ceiling.
*   **Gimmick:** Pausing the game or opening the inventory no longer stops time. Entities continue to move and attack while the player navigates the menu. The player must find a "Kill Command" item in their inventory and use it to unlock the exit door while being actively hunted.

---

## Floor 18: The Brightness Slider
*   **Atmosphere:** Total, absolute darkness. The only light comes from the player’s footsteps.
*   **Layout:** A pitch-black room with a "Blind Oracle" NPC at the entrance.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Settings Block:** A 1x1 block labeled "BRIGHTNESS."
    *   **The Light Node:** A glowing socket in the center of the room.
*   **Gimmick:** Pushing the "Settings Block" into the "Light Node" literally increases the game’s screen tint (brightness). However, there is a **Beast of Silence** that hunts by sound. Increasing the brightness also increases the "Noise" the player makes, making them easier to track.

---

## Floor 19: Erasing the Save
*   **Atmosphere:** Desperate and final. The sound of a delete-key clicking echoes periodically.
*   **Layout:** A corridor blocked by a "Wall of Corrupted Data."
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Save File Block:** A 2x2 block that looks like a 3.5" floppy disk.
    *   **The Erase Terminal:** A glowing red pit.
*   **Gimmick:** The player must **Push** the "Save File Block" into the "Erase Terminal." This action permanently removes the "Save" and "Load" options from the pause menu for the remainder of Stage 2. Return by Death becomes the only way to track progress.

---

## Floor 20: Checkpoint 4 — The Architect of Atrophy Encounter
*   **Atmosphere:** The Awakening Room (Phase 3: Atrophy) is here. Bloodstains on the floor, discarded weapons, and echoing death screams.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Mind Bar:** The boss (The Architect) has a bar that depletes based on the player's "Sacrifice."
*   **Gimmick:** The Architect drains **Mind** resource by 5 per second. The player must push three **Mechanical Sacrifice Blocks** into terminals to freeze the drain. Each block sacrifices a mechanic: **Sprint**, **Pause Menu Access**, or the **In-Battle Item Menu**.
*   **Victory:** Refusing the Architect's offer to "Restore" these mechanics (which would cause instant death) depletes its bar and grants access to the Anchor.
