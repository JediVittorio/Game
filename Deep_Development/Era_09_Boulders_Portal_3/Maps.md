# Era 9: Boulder's Portal 3 — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Sandbox of Thirst
Era 9 is a world of infinite choices, none of which matter as much as who the player is currently dating. The aesthetic is high-definition fantasy—lush forests, intricate stone ruins, and campfires that burn with the heat of a thousand romantic subplots. The air smells like lavender, expensive wine, and desperation.

---

## 1. The Crash Site: The Ravaged Beach of Indecision
A coastline littered with the wreckage of a massive, organic-looking ship (the Nautiloid-ish). The sand is suspiciously clean, and the sun is always at the perfect "golden hour" angle for a dramatic introduction.

### Map 9.1: The Wreckage of the Brain-Ship
*   **Atmosphere:** Sounds of crashing waves and the occasional "squelch" of a dying brain-parasite.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Dying Mind-Flayer:** An NPC who tries to telepathically seduce the player before dying of embarrassment.
    *   **The First D20 Pedestal:** A stone pillar where the player must roll for "Initiative to Walk."
*   **NPC Placement:** **Darkheart** is banging her head against a locked pod, reciting her tragic backstory to an audience of seagulls.

---

## 2. The Social Hub: The Thirsty Druid Grove
A hidden valley protected by massive stone walls and a very judgmental circle of druids. It is less of a sanctuary and more of a speed-dating venue.

### Map 9.2: The Camp of Polyamorous Tension
*   **Atmosphere:** Cozy, with multiple tents arranged in a circle. The background music is a seductive lute track that never stops.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Romance Meter Bar:** A literal bar in the center of the camp that displays the "Thirst Level" of every companion.
    *   **The Magic Mirror:** Where the player spends 45 real-world minutes changing their eyebrow thickness.
*   **Gameplay:** This is a "Social Dungeon." The player must navigate camp conversations to avoid accidentally entering into a legally binding marriage with a bear.

---

## 3. The Path to the Absolute: The Shadow-Cursed Pub
A gloomy, dark-fantasy landscape where the shadows are literally made of "Relationship Baggage."

### Map 9.3: The Last Light-ish Inn
*   **Atmosphere:** Oppressive darkness held back by a single, flickering lantern. Smells of damp wood and unrequited love.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The "Inspiration" Well:** A fountain that gives the player a "Reroll" token if they can recite a poem about their own trauma.
    *   **The ISO-Standard Bed:** A sleeping area where the characters argue over the geometric efficiency of their cuddle puddle.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles with "Shadow-Cursed Exes" and "Thirsty Goblins."

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Polyamorous Pit (Final Arena)
A massive, multidimensional arena located at the center of the brain-god’s consciousness. It is filled with heart-shaped platforms and floating roses.

### Map 9.4: Floor 1 — The Hall of Unspoken Feelings
*   **Atmosphere:** Romantic and terrifying. The walls are covered in "Approval Rating" graphs.
*   **Gimmick:** "The Critical Failure Maze." The player must roll a d20 every 10 steps. A natural 1 teleports the party back to the start of the relationship.

### Map 9.5: The Brain-God’s Boudoir (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A swirling vortex of purple neurons and red silk.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Throne of Thirst:** Where the **Absolute Heartbreaker** sits, holding a giant d20.
    *   **The Romance Orbs:** Floating spheres that, if destroyed, deal massive "Breakup Damage" to the boss.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The boss doesn't attack health; he attacks "Reputation." If the player's reputation with their party falls to zero, the companions join the boss's polycule and attack the player.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Tactical Sandbox
*   **Aesthetic:** A 3D-feeling top-down view with high-fidelity textures. Objects like barrels, crates, and chandeliers are everywhere, begging to be used in a "Creative Combo."
*   **Background:** Shifting d20 values and scrolling lines of "Relationship Approval" text.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Mind-Flayer Simps," "Romantic Revenants," and "Goblins with Guitars."
*   **Gimmick:** "The Bonus Action." The player can use a bonus action to "Flirt," which has a 5% chance to stun the enemy with confusion.
