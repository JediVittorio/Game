# Era 9: Boulder's Portal 3 — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Thirsty Travelers)

### 1. The Oathbreaker (The Accidental Sinner)
*   **Visuals:** A towering paladin in heavy, dark-iron armor. His helmet has a single, comically large "X" scratched over the holy symbol. He carries a massive "Gavel of Guilt" and a stolen silver spoon tucked into his belt.
*   **Personality:** He is deeply depressed and formal. He accidentally broke his holy oath not by committing an atrocity, but by absentmindedly pocketing a municipal spoon during a diplomatic dinner. He now views himself as the greatest villain in history and speaks in hushed, dramatic tones about "The Darkness of the Cutlery Drawer."
*   **Narrative Role:** The "Tank" and moral compass (which is spinning wildly). He provides high physical damage and constant, unnecessary apologies.
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Lay on Hands" ability has been renamed to "Awkward Pat on the Back," which heals 5 HP and inflicts "Mild Discomfort."

### 2. Darkheart (The Cliché Shadow)
*   **Visuals:** A shadow rogue with white hair, pale skin, and a face that is 90% "Mysterious Scowl." She wears leather armor with way too many straps and carries two daggers called "Tragedy" and "Exposition."
*   **Personality:** She is aggressively withholding about her backstory, constantly reminding the player that she has a "dark secret" that she will only reveal if the player gives her exactly three orchids and pets the camp dog. Her secret is that she once tripped in public and has never recovered from the shame.
*   **Narrative Role:** The primary DPS and source of "Edgy" dialogue. She exists to be romanced and to complain about the lack of shadows in the "golden hour" lighting.
*   **Satirical Twist:** Her "Sneak Attack" deals triple damage, but only if the target is currently talking about their feelings.

## Major NPCs

### 3. The Absolute Heartbreaker (The Boss)
*   **Description:** A massive, multidimensional mind-flayer god wearing a silk bathrobe and holding a glass of expensive red wine. He has tentacles that are perfectly manicured.
*   **Role:** The "Final Boss." He represents the absurdity of the game’s pervasive romantic aggression.
*   **Dialogue:** "At last, you arrive! Have you come to defeat me... or have you come to join my ever-expanding web of complicated emotional entanglements? Roll for Charisma, little snack! My approval rating is currently 'Thirsty'!"

### 4. Withers-ish (The Bone-Dry Narrator)
*   **Description:** An ancient, desiccated skeleton sitting in the player’s camp, wearing a tattered robe and reading a "Relationship Guide for Dummies."
*   **Role:** The person who allows the player to respec their characters and revive dead companions (for a "nominal fee").
*   **Dialogue:** "Thou hast returned... and thou art still single? Thy lack of romantic progress is... noteworthy. Shall I revive thy fallen companion? It will cost thee 200 gold and thine own dignity."

### 5. The Thirsty Mind-Flayer
*   **Description:** A dying illithid pinned under a piece of debris. It uses its telepathic powers to project images of it and the player holding hands on a beach.
*   **Role:** The "First Contact" NPC. He sets the tone for the era’s "romantic" focus.
*   **Dialogue:** "(Telepathically) *Join... me... in the... eternal... polycule... We could... share... a... brain... and... a... Pinterest... board...*"

## Common Enemies (The Romantic Revenants)

*   **Mind-Flayer Simp:** A lesser illithid that attacks by trying to write the player "Mental Love Letters" (deals Psychic damage).
*   **Shadow-Cursed Ex:** A ghost that attacks by bringing up things the player did in Era 1 that they thought everyone had forgotten.
*   **Thirsty Goblin:** A small, green creature with a guitar. It doesn't attack with weapons; it attacks with "Terrible Serenades."
*   **The Disapproving Druid:** A man who turns into a bear and then complains that the player didn't pet him correctly.
*   **ISO-Standard Skeleton:** A skeleton that attacks with "Geometric Efficiency," only striking on specific grid coordinates.
