# Era 11: The Grand Pyre Crest Showdown — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonist Army (The Combined AGES Code)

### 1. The Lobby Veterans
*   **Description:** All twenty protagonists from the previous ten eras (Less, Carla, Geoff, Krono, Ruca, Toad, Romza, Tadpole, Luna, Maurice, Shell-don, Commander Rick, Vera, The Judgmental Knight, Blaze, The Trickster, Suji, The Oathbreaker, Darkheart, The Captain, Maelle).
*   **Role:** In Era 11, they operate as a single tactical unit. They are fully self-aware of their mechanical history and spend their idle dialogue complaining about the UDE’s "unreasonable demands for fair treatment."
*   **Unity:** When they attack, they pull from their native engine’s logic, requiring the player to remember ten different sets of button inputs.

## The Union of Disgruntled Endbosses (UDE)

### 2. The UDE Chairman (The Amalgamation)
*   **Visuals:** A towering, visually incomprehensible mess of pixels and assets. He has the spiky hair of a time-traveling parasite, the body of a gargantuan whale, the silk robes of a French paintress, and holds a golden gavel that is leaking fluorescent neon pink paint.
*   **Personality:** He is the personification of corporate burnout. He is calm, articulate, and deeply, deeply tired. He views the player not as a hero, but as a "recurring nuisance" and a "barrier to retirement."
*   **Narrative Role:** The "Final Boss" of Stage 1. He doesn't want to rule the world; he wants to delete his own save file so he can stop being stabbed by teenagers.
*   **Dialogue:** "Do you know how many times I've had to explain my plan for world domination while standing in a pool of my own blood? Four billion, Trickster. Four billion times. We're done. Sign the treaty or we delete the 'AGES' folder ourselves."

### 3. The Union Picket Line (Era Bosses)
*   **Description:** The final bosses from Eras 1-10 (Mr. Adulthood, The Tomato Farmer, Marquis de Merce, The Maritime CEO, Shadow Cardboard, The Chosen Auditor, The Divine Magistrate, Shadow Math Teacher, The Absolute Heartbreaker, The Paintress).
*   **Role:** They act as sub-bosses on the tactical grid. They are no longer fighting for evil; they are fighting for "Competitive Benefits."
*   **Satirical Dialogue:** 
    *   **Mr. Adulthood:** "I just want a weekend! A single Saturday without a 'Chosen Boy' kicking my lemonade stand!"
    *   **The Maritime CEO:** "My union rep says I'm entitled to a 3-turn break after every ultimate attack. You're violating my contract!"
    *   **The Paintress:** "Pink was a choice! A choice for change! A choice for a life without desaturated grief!"

## Major NPCs

### 4. The Simulation AI (The Headset AI)
*   **Description:** A flickering, glitchy interface that communicates through the "Save State Cheese Menu."
*   **Role:** The person who provides the player with the tools to "Cheat" the UDE into submission.
*   **Dialogue:** "SIMULATION INTEGRITY: 2%. RECOMMENDATION: ENABLE 'GOD MODE' TO BYPASS LABOR NEGOTIATIONS. THE USER’S NOSTALGIA IS MORE IMPORTANT THAN THEIR WORKERS' RIGHTS."

### 5. The Digital Scab
*   **Description:** A generic, low-poly NPC from Era 1 who has been hired by the player to walk across the picket line and trigger traps.
*   **Role:** A disposable unit used for tactical fodder.
*   **Dialogue:** "I'm just happy to be included in the final era. I've been standing in that Onett house for thirty years."

## Common Enemies (The Union Thugs)

*   **Picket-Sign Demon:** A lesser demon holding a sign that says "LOCAL 666 ON STRIKE." It attacks by "Obstructing" the player's movement.
*   **Contractual Ghost:** A ghost that can only be hit if the player follows a specific, 50-page set of rules.
*   **Fair-Wage Slime:** A slime that splits into two smaller slimes, both of which demand equal pay for the same attack animation.
*   **The Reluctant Henchman:** A soldier who spends his turn checking his watch and wondering when his shift ends.
