# Era 2: Temporal Lever — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Shifting Timelines
Era 2 is a world defined by the "Butterfly Effect" gone horribly wrong. The aesthetics shift from vibrant 16-bit medievalism to prehistoric jungles, and finally to a "utopian" future that is aggressively red and round.

---

## 1. 1000 AD: The Millennium Fair of Mediocrity
A celebration of one thousand years of not having anything better to do. The map is filled with colorful tents, balloons that never pop, and a soundtrack that is an endlessly looping, slightly off-key trumpet fanfare.

### Map 2.1: The Teleporter Square
*   **Atmosphere:** Smells of ozone and overpriced fried dough.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The "Reliable" Teleporter:** A machine made of brass and hope. It hums with a sound that suggests it might explode at any moment.
    *   **The Bell of Judgment:** A giant bell that players can hit to determine their "heroic worth." It always rings a dull thud.
*   **NPC Placement:** **Ruca** is frantically recalibrating the machine while a crowd of generic villagers waits for something—anything—to happen.

---

## 2. 65,000,000 BC: The Stomping Grounds
A lush, prehistoric jungle where the primary activity is accidentally altering the future.

### Map 2.2: The Insect Glade
*   **Atmosphere:** Humid, vibrant, and buzzing with the sound of destiny.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Single Prehistoric Bug:** A gold-colored beetle that crawls in a very specific, non-threatening pattern. Stomping it triggers the "Tomato Cascade."
    *   **The Primitive Hut:** A dwelling made of dinosaur bones and giant leaves. It belongs to a cavewoman who is surprisingly well-versed in temporal mechanics.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles here are with "Aggressive Ferns" and "Existential Lizards."

---

## 3. 2300 AD: The Tomato Utopia
Because of the bug-stomping incident, humans never evolved. Instead, tomatoes achieved sentience, bipedalism, and a very high standard of living.

### Map 2.3: New Orchard City
*   **Atmosphere:** A hyper-clean, futuristic city rendered in shades of red, green, and silver. The air smells like fresh salsa.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Juice Bar:** The social hub where bipedal tomatoes discuss philosophy and irrigation techniques.
    *   **The Monument to the Stomped Bug:** A massive golden statue of the beetle from 65M BC, worshipped as the "Great Catalyst."
*   **NPC Placement:** **Tomato Citizens** roll around on hover-disks, wearing tiny hats.

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Agricultural Abyss
A massive, high-tech farming facility where the "Apocalyptic Parasite" (now a farmer) resides.

### Map 2.4: Floor 1 — The Fertile Fields
*   **Atmosphere:** Glowing hydroponic tubes and the sound of classical music being played to sentient seedlings.
*   **Gimmick:** The "Pollination Puzzle." The player must use **Ruca’s** Fire tech to clear "Agricultural Blight" (which is actually just normal weeds) to open the automated gates.

### Map 2.5: The Master Silo (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A vast, cylindrical chamber filled with the highest quality fertilizer in the galaxy.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Hover-Tractor:** The boss’s primary vehicle.
    *   **The Greenhouse Dome:** Looks out into a future where everything is perfect, which is the ultimate motivation for the player to destroy it.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The floor is slippery (due to juice). The player must use **Toad’s** Slime tech to create traction points.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Temporal Rift
*   **Aesthetic:** Shifting tilesets that rapidly cycle between jungle, medieval, and futuristic elements.
*   **Background:** A swirling blue-and-white vortex with giant clock gears floating in the distance.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Time Wraiths," "Paradox Poodles," and "Sentient Fertilizer."
