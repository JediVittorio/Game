# Era 7: Deity: Initial Iniquity 2 — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Continent of Causal Chaos
Era 7 is a world where physics is not a set of laws, but a series of explosive suggestions. The aesthetic is high-fantasy isometric—verdant forests, crumbling stone castles, and pristine beaches—all of which are destined to be covered in "Necrofire" within thirty seconds of the player’s arrival. The air smells like sulfur, ozone, and wet dog.

---

## 1. Fort Sad-ness: The Prison Colony
A crumbling stone fortress surrounded by a shallow, muddy moat. It is populated by "Sourcerers" (people who accidentally set their kitchens on fire) and the Magisters who "correct" them.

### Map 7.1: The Ghetto of Broken Vases
*   **Atmosphere:** Damp and depressing. Every corner contains a vase that is begging to be smashed.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Cooking Pot of Destiny:** A mundane cooking station that serves as the primary source of environmental disasters.
    *   **The Black Pits (Miniature):** A small puddle of oil that looks harmless but is strategically placed next to a torch.
*   **NPC Placement:** **The Judgmental Knight** is standing by the main gate, staring intensely at a cat to see if it’s casting spells.

---

## 2. Drift-wood: The Town of Trivial Tragedies
A bustling harbor town built on a series of wooden docks. It is a masterpiece of architectural insecurity, given how many people in town possess fire magic.

### Map 7.2: The Tavern of Volatile Spirits
*   **Atmosphere:** Rowdy. Smells of cheap ale and impending litigation.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Beer Barrels:** Hundreds of barrels of highly flammable liquid, stacked precariously against the walls.
    *   **The "Static" Rug:** A rug in the center of the room that applies "Shocked" to anyone who walks over it.
*   **Gameplay:** A non-combat map where the player must navigate dialogue to avoid starting a chain reaction that would erase the town from the map.

---

## 3. The Nameless Isle: The Elemental Sandbox
A tropical island where every square inch of terrain is a different elemental surface. It looks like a giant, dangerous quilt.

### Map 7.3: The Surface Puzzle Glade
*   **Atmosphere:** Vibrant and humming with magical energy.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Blood Fountain:** A fountain that periodically sprays blood, turning the ground into a slippery, conductive mess.
    *   **The Steam Vent:** A pipe that emits clouds of steam, which can be turned into Necrofire Steam by the mere presence of the party.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles here involve "Environmental Slimes" and "Surface Spirits."

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Cathedral of the Divine Ego
A massive, white-marble cathedral that is far too clean for this world. It is the residence of the final boss, Lucian-ish.

### Map 7.4: Floor 1 — The Hall of Unintended Consequences
*   **Atmosphere:** Grand and echoing. The floor is so polished it reflects the player’s sins.
*   **Gimmick:** "The Chain Reaction." The room is filled with interconnected pipes of water, oil, and poison. The player must use **Blaze’s** fire to trigger a *controlled* explosion to open the door, without killing the party.

### Map 7.5: The Ascension Throne (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A floating platform in the middle of a vortex of pure "Source" energy.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The God-King’s Mirror:** Reflects the player’s elemental attacks back as "Necrofire."
    *   **The Elemental Reservoirs:** Massive vats of liquid that the boss can tip over to change the arena’s surface.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The boss, **The Divine Magistrate**, changes his elemental resistance every turn. The player must use **The Judgmental Knight’s** "Bless" to clear the surfaces before they stack into a continent-ending explosion.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Elemental Crucible
*   **Aesthetic:** An isometric platform that is 50% surface and 50% "Soon-to-be-Surface."
*   **Background:** Swirling clouds of Necrofire Steam and raining blood.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Magister Karens," "Necro-Rats," and "Void-Woken Interns."
*   **Gimmick:** "The Surface Combo." Standing in water makes you vulnerable to electricity. Standing in fire makes you vulnerable to... everything.
