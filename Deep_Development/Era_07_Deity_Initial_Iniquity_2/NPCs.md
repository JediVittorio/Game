# Era 7: Deity: Initial Iniquity 2 — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Elemental Extremists)

### 1. The Judgmental Knight (The Inquisitor)
*   **Visuals:** A man in heavy, white-and-gold plate armor that is far too shiny for a prison colony. He wears a hood that obscures his eyes and carries a massive "Mace of Morality."
*   **Personality:** He is an inquisitor who views every minor magical act as a "Capital Source Offense." He believes that "Wet" status is a gateway drug to "Necrofire." He speaks in a deep, booming voice and is prone to executing inanimate objects (like chairs) for "suspected heretical vibrations."
*   **Narrative Role:** The "hero" who provides physical crowd control. He doesn't just attack; he "Adjudicates."
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Bless" ability has a 50% chance to turn a surface into "Holy Water" and a 50% chance to turn it into "Slightly Less Bad Fire."

### 2. Blaze (The Pyromancer)
*   **Visuals:** A skeletal-looking man with glowing orange eyes and hair that is literally on fire. He wears a robe made of asbestos and carries a staff that is just a burning log.
*   **Personality:** Blaze views the world as a fundamental deficiency of fire. He is helpful, polite, and absolutely insane. He believes that the solution to every problem—from a locked door to a complex political dispute—is "More Necrofire."
*   **Narrative Role:** The primary damage dealer. He doesn't just cast spells; he initiates "Thermodynamic Reconstructions."
*   **Satirical Twist:** He has a passive ability called "The Sneeze," where any movement has a 5% chance to ignite the ground behind him.

## Major NPCs

### 3. The Divine Magistrate (The Boss)
*   **Description:** A tall, incredibly handsome man with long flowing blonde hair and wings made of pure, blindingly white light. He carries a golden gavel.
*   **Role:** The "Final Boss." He represents the hubris of assuming one can control the chaos of the world.
*   **Dialogue:** "I have ascended to godhood to bring order to this continent! But you... you keep boiling tea! Do you realize how many timelines you’ve erased by making a sandwich?"

### 4. Malady-ish (The Cryptic Guide)
*   **Description:** A red-skinned demon-woman with horns and a very revealing leather outfit. She smokes a pipe that emits "Source Smoke."
*   **Role:** The person who provides the player with a ship and a series of vague, unhelpful warnings.
*   **Dialogue:** "The Void is coming, darling. Or maybe it’s just the smell of your pyromancer. Either way, you should probably go kill a god. It’ll be fun."

### 5. The Necro-Rat
*   **Description:** A standard rat that has been exposed to so much Necrofire that it has become immortal and very, very angry.
*   **Role:** A recurring "guide" who gives the player tips on how to maximize environmental destruction.
*   **Dialogue:** "(Squeak) Why use a sword when you can use a puddle? I’ve seen empires fall because someone spilled their wine on a lightning bolt."

## Common Enemies (The Causal Victims)

*   **Magister Karen:** A low-level guard with a "Can I speak to your manager?" attitude. She attacks by "Reporting" the player to the Divine, dealing psychological damage.
*   **Void-Woken Intern:** A creature from the Void that is just here for the experience. It attacks with "Unpaid Labor" (drains MP).
*   **Necro-Chicken:** A chicken that exploded and then got back up. It leaves a trail of Necrofire wherever it walks.
*   **Environmental Slime:** A creature made of whatever surface it is currently standing on. It changes elements every turn.
