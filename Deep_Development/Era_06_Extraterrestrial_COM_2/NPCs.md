# Era 6: Extraterrestrial-COM 2 — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Squad of Psychological Instability)

### 1. Commander Rick (The Recon Ranger)
*   **Visuals:** A man in tactical armor that has seen better days. He wears a tattered green beret and has a constant, twitching eye. He carries a shotgun and a "Emotional Support Sword."
*   **Personality:** Rick is the leader of the resistance, which mostly means he is the person who tells the other soldiers that it’s "totally normal" to miss a shot from two inches away. He communicates in clipped military jargon and frequently pours his whiskey directly into his eyes "to improve his hit chance."
*   **Narrative Role:** The "hero" who manages the party's morale. He doesn't give orders; he gives "Suggestions of Success."
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Special Move" is "Total Gaslight," where he convinces a panicked soldier that they actually *wanted* to miss that 99% shot because it was a "tactical diversion."

### 2. Vera (The Field Specialist)
*   **Visuals:** A woman in high-tech medic gear, perpetually covered in shrapnel wounds and bandages. She carries a drone that looks like it’s also suffering from depression.
*   **Personality:** Vera is the party's medic, but she is so traumatized by the losses she’s seen that her healing mostly consists of saying "There, there, at least you’re still mathematically alive." She is the only person who acknowledges that the aliens are actually just as confused as the humans.
*   **Narrative Role:** The healer/buffer. She uses her drone to provide "Hope" (a stat that is immediately drained by any RNG failure).
*   **Satirical Twist:** Her "Ultimate Heal" has a 1% chance to accidentally summon an alien strike team because she "lost focus while contemplating the futility of war."

## Major NPCs

### 3. The Chosen Auditor (The Boss)
*   **Description:** A tall, slender alien in an expensive-looking suit, carrying a glowing tablet and a clipboard.
*   **Role:** The "Final Boss." He represents the crushing reality of statistical probability.
*   **Dialogue:** "I’ve audited your last twelve turns, Commander. Your performance is... unacceptable. That 99% miss? That wasn't bad luck. That was a clerical error in your will to live. Let's fix that."

### 4. The Socially Awkward Sectoid
*   **Description:** A classic gray alien with a massive head and spindly limbs. It wears a tiny bow tie.
*   **Role:** A recurring antagonist who tries to use mind control but mostly just ends up sharing its own social anxieties with the player.
*   **Dialogue:** "(Psychic Whisper) Does... does this helmet make my head look too big? I was trying to mind-control you, but I forgot what I was going to say. Let's just... both walk away?"

### 5. Central-ish (The Voice of Anxiety)
*   **Description:** A voice that speaks over the radio (and occasionally appears as a hologram). He is obsessed with "Objectives" and "Extraction."
*   **Role:** The narrator of the era's stress.
*   **Dialogue:** "Commander! The aliens are making progress on the Avatar-ish Project! We need to extract immediately! Or stay and fight! Or hide in a box! Just do something that is 100% successful for once!"

## Common Enemies (The Statistical Anomalies)

*   **ADVENT Grunt:** A brainwashed human soldier in sleek red-and-black armor. They attack in groups and strictly follow "The Procedure," which usually involves standing in the open and getting shot.
*   **Panic-Inducing Drone:** A small, flying sphere that emits a high-pitched sound. It doesn't deal damage; it just increases the player's "Panic" meter.
*   **Statistical Nightmare:** A creature made of floating numbers and exclamation points. It attacks by "Subtracting" the player’s hit chance.
*   **The Overly Honest Alien:** A soldier who tells you exactly where its teammates are hiding because it "disagrees with the management's lack of transparency."
