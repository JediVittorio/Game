# Era 6: Extraterrestrial-COM 2 — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Grid of Despair
Era 6 is a world of sharp angles, high-tech neon, and the constant, looming threat of a 99% miss. The aesthetic is "Military Dystopia"—slick black surfaces, glowing blue interfaces, and tactical cover that looks like it was placed by a confused interior designer. The air smells like ozone and panic.

---

## 1. The Mobile Base: The Avenger-ish (The Therapy Hub)
A massive, repurposed alien transport ship that serves as the resistance's headquarters. It is less of a military command center and more of a high-security counseling facility.

### Map 6.1: The Group Therapy Deck
*   **Atmosphere:** Sounds of rhythmic breathing exercises and the soft hum of the ship’s engines. The lighting is a calming "Safe-Space Blue."
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Circle of Chairs:** Where the soldiers gather to discuss their "missed-shot trauma."
    *   **The Gaslighting Terminal:** Where **Commander Rick** monitors the psychological state of his troops and feeds them "Positive Reinforcement" (lies).
    *   **The Memorial Wall:** A screen that displays the names of every soldier who died because a teammate panicked over a missed shot.
*   **NPC Placement:** **Commander Rick** is staring at a tactical display that currently shows a 0% chance of success for everything.

---

## 2. ADVENT-ish City Center: The Neon Prison
A hyper-clean, futuristic city where the inhabitants are monitored by floating cameras and the billboard signs exclusively show happy aliens and sad humans.

### Map 6.2: The High-Security Plaza
*   **Atmosphere:** Tense. The hum of drones and the rhythmic marching of ADVENT boots.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Half-Cover Benches:** Stone benches placed perfectly at 1.5-meter intervals.
    *   **The Surveillance Monolith:** A giant screen that tracks the "Happiness Level" of the citizens. It is currently at -40%.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles here are cover-based. Bypassing a camera triggers a "Reinforcement" spawn.

---

## 3. The Procedural Forest: The 99% Zone
A dense, foggy forest where the trees look identical and the lines of sight are intentionally confusing.

### Map 6.3: The Extraction Point
*   **Atmosphere:** Claustrophobic. Smells of pine needles and gun-smoke.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The "Flankable" Trees:** Large trunks that provide Full Cover but are susceptible to being destroyed by a single errant grenade.
    *   **The Skyranger Signal:** A glowing green beacon that marks the end of the mission.
*   **Gimmick:** This is a "Timed" map. The player has 12 turns to reach the signal before the "Mental Breakdown" meter maxes out.

---

## 4. The Dungeon: The Alien Brain-God Fortress
A sprawling complex of purple energy and floating platforms. It is designed to shatter the human psyche through sheer statistical impossibility.

### Map 6.4: Floor 1 — The Hall of Probability
*   **Atmosphere:** Silent, except for the sound of digital dice rolling in the distance.
*   **Gimmick:** "The RNG Gauntlet." Every step the player takes triggers a "Probability Check." If the check fails, a random debuff is applied to the party.

### Map 6.5: The Core of the Chosen (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A vast, circular chamber with a floor that shifts between a 3D grid and a circle of folding chairs.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Statistical Throne:** Where the **Chosen Auditor** sits, calculating the player's defeat.
    *   **The UI Shatter-Points:** Floating hexagonal screens that the boss can destroy to disable the player’s ability to see their own stats.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The boss doesn't attack health; he attacks "Probability." He can "Force a Miss" for the next three turns, requiring the player to use **Vera’s** "Emotional Stability" buffs to counteract the inevitable panic.

---

## 5. Battle Maps: The Tactical Grid
*   **Aesthetic:** A glowing blue hexagonal grid overlaid on the map environment.
*   **Background:** Shifting tactical readouts, soldier status bars, and the giant word "MISS" flashing in the distance.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "ADVENT Grunts," "Socially Awkward Sectoids," and "Panic-Inducing Drones."
*   **Gimmick:** "The Cover Mechanic." Standing behind an object provides a Defense bonus, but also makes the soldier feel "Protected" (a temporary morale buff).
