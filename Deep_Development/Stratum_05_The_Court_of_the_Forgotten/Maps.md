# Stratum 5: The Court of the Forgotten — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Decaying Courtroom
Stratum 5 is a twisted, legalistic interpretation of the protagonist's repressed justifications and excuses. The aesthetic is "Decaying Courtroom"—massive mahogany benches, marble pillars that are crumbling into dust, and scales that are perpetually unbalanced. The air smells like old parchment, sealing wax, and wood rot. The ground is covered in a layer of "Evidence"—discarded items and photographs from the protagonist's life.

---

## Floor 21: The Gatekeeper
*   **Atmosphere:** Strict and exclusionary. The sound of a gavel slamming echoes every 10 seconds.
*   **Layout:** A narrow corridor leading to a massive, brass-bound door guarded by the **Gatekeeper NPC**.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Frame-Lock:** A keypad on the door that accepts a 4-digit code.
*   **Gimmick:** The password changes every Return by Death cycle and is mathematically tied to the exact frame the player entered the floor. It is essentially impossible to "Guess." The player must strike the Gatekeeper to initiate a 5-minute evasion puzzle. Winning yields the door; losing (death) reveals the pattern on the death screen for the next loop.

---

## Floor 22: The Jester's Trial
*   **Atmosphere:** Mocking and chaotic. The background music is a distorted, carnival-like version of a legal march.
*   **Layout:** A large hall filled with "Justification Blocks"—pushable slabs that must be arranged into a specific geometric shape to bridge a gap.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Jester NPC:** An entity that actively "un-pushes" the player's blocks in real-time.
*   **Gimmick:** The player must "Kill" the Jester on Loop 1 to learn the geometric solution in peace. Then, Return by Death back and solve the puzzle while the Jester disrupts them, using the knowledge of the solution to work faster than the disruption.

---

## Floor 23: The Liar's Paradox
*   **Atmosphere:** Tense and dualistic. The room is divided by a line of white and black tiles.
*   **Layout:** A symmetrical room with two identical NPCs and two identical doors.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Twin Witnesses:** Two NPCs who both claim to be "Truth" and "Lies."
*   **Gimmick:** Both doors lead to instant death on Loop 1. The only way to progress is to "Kill" one of the NPCs. The survivor—traumatized by witnessing the player's act of violence—will reveal a hidden third door in sheer terror. Safety is found only through the witness of violence.

---

## Floor 24: The Guillotine
*   **Atmosphere:** Fatalistic and silent. The lighting is focused entirely on the center of the room.
*   **Layout:** A long walk toward a massive, wooden guillotine structure.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Executioner NPC:** Reads the player's "Crimes" (DEATH_COUNT, NPCs killed, etc.).
*   **Gimmick:** A scripted capture cutscene occurs where the player is placed in the guillotine. On Loop 1, the blade falls (Return by Death). On Loop 2, the player must use the **Rusted Sword** (from Floor 23's violence) to jam the blade tracks. Freedom is available only to those who remember they already died.

---

## Floor 25: Checkpoint 5 — Sovereign of Cognitive Dissonance
*   **Atmosphere:** The Awakening Room is gone. Instead, the checkpoint bed is surrounded by the ghost apparitions of every NPC the player struck or sacrificed. They whisper constantly.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Contradiction Aura:** The Sovereign rules here. Every menu action displays the wrong result.
*   **Gimmick:** The Sovereign encounter. The player must use the **Speak** verb (the only one that works correctly) to persuade six "Comedic Archetypes" from Stage 1 to accept their Stage 2 truth.
*   **The Sacrifice:** The player must willingly and permanently surrender the **HP Bar UI** display during this encounter. From this point on, combat is played with no visible health indicator.
