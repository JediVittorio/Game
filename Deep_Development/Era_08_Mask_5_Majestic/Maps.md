# Era 8: Mask 5: Majestic — Detailed Map Specifications
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## Overview: The Cognitive High School
Era 8 transitions between the mundane reality of a high school student and the hyper-stylized, high-contrast world of the "Cognitive Palace." The aesthetic is urban modern meets psychedelic rebellion. Reality is filled with gray tones and bustling crowds, while the Palace is a riot of red, black, and gold—until the "Geometry Glitch" turns it into a neon-lined math exam.

---

## 1. Reality: Shujin-ish High School
A sprawling, concrete structure where the primary hazard is the "Schedule."

### Map 8.1: The Classroom of Crushing Boredom
*   **Atmosphere:** Smells of chalk dust and chalked-up expectations. The background music is a low-fi hip-hop track that loops every 15 seconds.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Protagonist’s Desk:** Located at the back-left, next to the window. It is the only place where the "Fuzzy Memory" is clear.
    *   **The Chalkboard:** Currently displays a complex equation that physically radiates a "Confusion" aura.
*   **NPC Placement:** **The Math Teacher** stands at the front, his shadow occasionally stretching into a multi-limbed monster.

### Map 8.2: The Beef Bowl Shop (Minimum Wage Dungeon)
*   **Atmosphere:** Greasy, cramped, and humid. The sound of sizzling meat and aggressive customer demands.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Ordering Kiosk:** A terminal that requires the player to input complex combos to "Attack" orders.
    *   **The "Social Stat" Corner:** Where the player sits to increase "Knowledge" but inevitably loses "Sanity."
*   **Gameplay:** This is a "Job" map. Failure to serve customers correctly results in an "Economic Penalty" to the player's gold.

---

## 2. The Metaverse: The Palace of Rationalization
Initially a lavish casino, but transformed by the protagonist’s failing grades into a nightmare of academic anxiety.

### Map 8.3: The Hall of Non-Euclidean Heists
*   **Atmosphere:** High-contrast red and black. The architecture ignores the laws of gravity—stairs lead to walls, and doors open into the ceiling.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Grappling Hook Points:** Glowing red anchors that allow the player to zip across gaps in the 3D-feeling 2D space.
    *   **The Shadow Patrols:** Enemies represented by floating, dark masks that explode into geometry when touched.
*   **Encounter Logic:** Battles use "Press-Turn" mechanics. Exploiting a weakness grants an "Extra Turn."

---

## 3. The Dungeon: The Geometry Exam Palace
A vast, ascending tower of floating protractors, graph-paper floors, and ink-well pits.

### Map 8.4: Floor 1 — The Calculus Corridor
*   **Atmosphere:** The music changes to a frantic, orchestral violin track. The walls are made of scrolling mathematical proofs.
*   **Gimmick:** "The Angle Trap." Certain tiles rotate the player’s directional inputs by 45, 90, or 180 degrees. The player must calculate their "Trajectory" to reach the exit.

### Map 8.5: The Summit of Failure (Boss Arena)
*   **Atmosphere:** A circular platform made of a giant, graded test paper (marked with a red 'F'). The sky is a vortex of falling red pens.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   **The Teacher’s Podium:** A towering structure where the boss looks down on the player.
    *   **The Compass Spears:** Giant, rotating needles that sweep the arena.
*   **Boss Map Logic:** The boss, **Shadow Math Teacher**, forces the player to solve a math problem every three turns. If the player misses the answer, they are "Detained" (Stunned) for two turns.

---

## 4. Battle Maps: The Cognitive Arena
*   **Aesthetic:** Stylized shadows, floating UI elements, and a background that "reacts" to the player’s actions.
*   **Background:** If the player exploits a weakness, the background flashes "1-MORE!" in giant graffiti letters.
*   **Encounters:** This is where the party fights "Aggressive Protractors," "Algebraic Demons," and "Parental Expectations."
*   **Gimmick:** "The All-Out Attack." If all enemies are downed, the background turns solid red with black silhouettes for a cinematic finisher.
