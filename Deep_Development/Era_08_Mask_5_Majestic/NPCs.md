# Era 8: Mask 5: Majestic — NPC Profiles
**Status:** Deep Development Initial Draft

## The Protagonists (The Phantom Midterm-Failures)

### 1. The Trickster (The Stylish Wild Card)
*   **Visuals:** A teenager in a sleek black trench coat with high collars and glowing red zippers. He wears a bird-like white mask and stylish glasses that don't actually have lenses. He carries a dagger and a "Handgun of Rebellion."
*   **Personality:** The Trickster is impossibly cool and silent. He communicates via stylish poses and the occasional "Hmph." He is a "Wild Card," meaning he can carry multiple mythological deities in his pockets, but he can't remember his 7x tables.
*   **Narrative Role:** The leader of the "Phantom Midterm-Failures." He is the one who "Awakens" to the realization that education is a construct of the adults to keep the teenagers from being too stylish.
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Persona" is called **Arsene-ish**, a gentleman thief whose primary crime is "Unpaid Student Loans."

### 2. Suji (The Delinquent Brawler)
*   **Visuals:** A boy with bleached blonde hair, a tattered school uniform, and a constant, aggressive scowl. He carries a lead pipe and a "Slingshot of Justice."
*   **Personality:** Suji constantly screams that he is "Not a delinquent!" while literally beating up a trash can. He is fiercely loyal but easily confused by anything more complex than a "Punch" command.
*   **Narrative Role:** The "best friend" and primary physical damage dealer. He provides the "Muscle" for the heists.
*   **Satirical Twist:** His "Persona Awakening" occurred when he realized he spent 40 hours "hanging out" with a girl at a library and didn't learn a single thing.

## Major NPCs

### 3. Shadow Math Teacher (The Boss)
*   **Description:** A monstrous version of the school’s math teacher. He has four arms, each holding a different geometric tool (a protractor, a compass, a ruler, and a red pen). He wears a toga made of graph paper.
*   **Role:** The "Final Boss" of the Palace. He represents the crushing authority of academic validation.
*   **Dialogue:** "You think style can solve for X? You think rebellion is a variable? Your life is an irrational number, Trickster! I shall round you down to zero!"

### 4. Igor-ish (The Long-Nosed Warden)
*   **Description:** An elderly man with a nose so long it has its own zip code. He sits in a blue-tinted elevator (The Velvet Room-ish) and giggles cryptically.
*   **Role:** The guide who helps the player "Execute" their Personas to create more powerful (and more indebted) ones.
*   **Dialogue:** "Welcome to my Velvet-ish Room. It’s a metaphor for your own mental imprisonment. Or maybe I just like the color blue. Either way, please sign this waiver for your own execution."

### 5. Morgana-ish (The Not-A-Cat)
*   **Description:** A small, black-and-white feline creature with a yellow bandana. He insists he is a human.
*   **Role:** The navigator who tells the player to "Go to sleep" every time they try to do something productive.
*   **Dialogue:** "Trickster! You’re looking tired! You should go to sleep! You have a palace to infiltrate tomorrow! And by tomorrow, I mean 30 real-world minutes of unskippable dialogue!"

## Common Enemies (The Cognitive Shadows)

*   **Aggressive Protractor:** A floating, semi-circular tool that attacks by "Measuring" the player’s weaknesses.
*   **Algebraic Demon:** A creature made of floating 'X's and 'Y's. It attacks by solving itself into a massive explosion.
*   **Parental Expectation:** A faceless shadow in a business suit that attacks by "Comparing" the player to their more successful cousin.
*   **Social Stat Wall:** A literal wall that prevents the player from progressing until they have "pet enough dogs" or "eaten enough beef bowls."
